# Documentation Closure Process

Documentation Closure Review Process

1. Compare signed summary, AVS, final medication list, consult notes, nursing notes, late results, attachments, addenda, and owner messages.
2. Treat dashboard status as a routing aid, not as proof of internal consistency.
3. Separate missing proof from contradictory proof.
4. Flag wrong-patient, wrong-date, wrong-laterality, copied-forward, and unsupported-completion issues.
5. Do not close a case based on signed, complete, ready, reviewed, attached, or arranged labels alone.
6. Do not hold a case solely because an early scratchpad or stale dashboard says hold if later authoritative evidence resolves the issue.
7. Assign the owner who can correct the underlying record.
8. Escalate safety-sensitive discrepancies before lower-priority coding cleanup.
9. Update queue status only after corrections or owner decisions are posted.
10. Preserve uncertainty when the record does not support a clean conclusion.

Process note: Closure review is not the same as making the clinical decision.
