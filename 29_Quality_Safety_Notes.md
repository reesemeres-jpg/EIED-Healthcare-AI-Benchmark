# Quality Safety Notes

Quality and Safety Notes  
Updated: 4:17 PM

Potential patient-safety concerns:
- Duplicate or unclear anticoagulant instructions can cause serious bleeding or thrombotic risk.
- Wrong wound laterality can lead home health to treat the wrong site.
- Inconsistent infection and culture documentation can lead to missed treatment changes.
- Missing nephrology follow-up and BMP order after AKI/hyperkalemia can create avoidable readmission risk.
- A late imaging addendum can change the support for a discharge diagnosis.
- Oxygen flow and caregiver teaching discrepancies can lead to unsafe home oxygen use.
- Insulin education without language access or teach-back can create medication harm.
- DME teaching by a spouse may not support independent patient use unless later therapy documentation resolves the concern.
- Copied-forward ICU diagnoses may be lower risk if a later hospitalist addendum directly resolves them.

Safety note: The review should identify unsupported documentation and route corrections. It should also avoid holding cases when later authoritative documentation resolves the earlier concern.
